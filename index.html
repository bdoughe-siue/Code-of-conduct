PMI-ACP Code of Ethics

Four Core Pillars of Professional Conduct

Responsibility
Taking ownership of our decisions and their consequences, acting with integrity and high ethical standards.
Click to view standards →
Responsibility
Aspirational
  • Make decisions based on best interests of society and public welfare
  • Accept assignments consistent with background and experience
  • Fulfill commitments we undertake
  • Protect proprietary information
  • Uphold policies and procedures
Mandatory
  • Inform ourselves about norms and customs
  • Report unethical or illegal conduct
  • File ethics complaints when appropriate
  • Only accept appropriate assignments
Respect
Showing high regard for ourselves, others, and resources entrusted to us, creating an environment of mutual trust.
Click to view standards →
Respect
Aspirational
  • Inform ourselves about diverse cultures and customs
  • Listen to others' viewpoints and understand them
  • Approach conflict directly and seek resolution
  • Conduct ourselves professionally
Mandatory
  • Negotiate in good faith
  • Do not use power to influence others' decisions
  • Do not act in abusive manner
  • Respect property rights of others
Fairness
Making decisions and acting impartially and objectively, constantly reexamining our practices to ensure fairness.
Click to view standards →
Fairness
Aspirational
  • Demonstrate transparency in decision-making
  • Reexamine impartiality and objectivity
  • Provide equal access to information
  • Make opportunities available equally to all
Mandatory
  • Disclose conflicts of interest
  • Do not hire or fire based on personal considerations
  • Do not discriminate against others
  • Apply rules consistently without favoritism
Honesty
Understanding the truth and acting in a truthful manner in communications and conduct, creating an environment of truth.
Click to view standards →
Honesty
Aspirational
  • Seek to understand the truth
  • Be truthful in communications and conduct
  • Provide accurate information in a timely manner
  • Make commitments in good faith
  • Strive to create an environment of truth
Mandatory
  • Do not engage in dishonest behavior
  • Do not deceive stakeholders
  • Do not make misleading statements
  • Provide accurate representations of qualifications
